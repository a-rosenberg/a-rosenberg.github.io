coconut peanut rice bowl

for when you've been eating unhealthy and need to feel good about yourself

ingredients

  • 1.5 cup short grain brown rice
  • 1/2 cup wild rice
  • 2 cup thinly sliced red cabbage
  • 1 Tbsp rice vinegar and 3 Tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 large asian sweet potato or 2 medium; 1" dice (normal sweet potatos are fine)
  • 1 small head of broccoli cut into florets
  • 1 carrot sliced into the thinnest rounds you can
  • 1 bunch of green onions sliced
  • 1 can full fat coconut milk
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 3 Tbsp thai red curry paste
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • toasted sesame seeds
recipe multiplier:

instructions

Mix the two rices in a rice cooker bowl then rinse, drain, and fill water again to the appropiate level. Start the rice cooker on the brown rice setting. Heat oven to 375f and place sweet potato on lightly oiled parchment paper covered cooking sheet. Season with salt and pepper. Once at heat, cook for 15 minutes without moving to create crisp bottoms while keeping vigilant to avoid burning. Flip and cook for another 5 minutes before removing. Add broccoli and season again, then return to the oven at 400f for 10 minutes. While the potatoes are cooking prepare the cabbage and sauce. Mix the cabbage in a small pinch of salt, sugar and 1 Tbsp of rice vinegar; toss occasionally. Combine coconut milk, peanut butter, curry paste, soy sauce and remaining 3 Tbsp rice vinegar in a small pot. Cook over low heat while stirring as much as possible to prevent the bottom from burning. After 5-10 minutes it should thicken significantly; at this point turn off the heat and set aside. Once the potatoes and broccoli are finished, assemble rice, roasted vegetables, cabbage, green onion, and sliced carrot and top with a few spoonfuls of the peanut sauce and sesame seeds. Optionally add a fried egg or sriracha for more heat.

notes

I find this achieves somewhat perfect color balance with the bright yolk of an over-easy egg. This doesn't cook fast because of the roasting and rice, but it is simple and can be done somewhat in the background for much of the cook time.
