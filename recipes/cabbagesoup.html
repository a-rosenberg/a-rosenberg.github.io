seigel's deli cabbage soup

my ultimate comfort food

ingredients

  • 5 cup cored green cabbage; 1/4" sliced
  • 1 yellow onion; chopped
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp ground fennel
  • 1/4 tsp ground cumin
  • 4 cup vegetable stock (best)
  • 14 oz can whole tomatos (skinless)
  • 1 loaf rye bread or a french boule for dipping
  • caraway seeds (optional)

instructions

Start by prepping the vegetables. Add the butter and oil together in a large pot over medium heat until the butter is almost completely melted. Add the onion and cook for 3-5 minutes until beginning to become transluscent. Add the spices and toss to coat onions then cook another 2-3 minutes until fragrant. Add the sliced cabbage and tomatoes then mix while cooking for another 2-3 minutes. At this point add the stock, bring to a boil, then down to a simmer for at least 30 minutes. You can cover the pot if you're losing too much liquid. Start checking the texture at around 30 minutes. Once the soup is cooked down to your liking, season with salt and pepper until all of the flavors come through but not so much that the soup is strongly salty. Ladel into bowls and top with a few caraway seeds (optional). Serve with lightly toasted, buttered rye bread or a crusty boule to ripped into pieces and dunk.

notes

With a recipe this simple make sure to get the best ingredients possible. I ground the fennel and cumin right before putting it in the pan for the most fragrant soup.
